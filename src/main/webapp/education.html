About Black Lives Matter

Black Lives Matter is an organized movement that advocates for civil protest against acts of systemic anti-Black racism and state-sanctioned violence that disproportionately affects Black lives. The founders Alice Garza, Patrisse Khan-Cullors, and Opal Tometi started the movement to connect all those with a shared interest to fight injustices against the Black community and to create a space inclusive to all Black Lives.


#BlackLivesMatter emerged in 2013 as a call to action on social media in response to the acquittal of Trayvon Martin’s murderer. Upon the deaths of Mike Brown and Eric Garner at the hands of law enforcement in 2014, the movement progressed from social media to physical demonstrations thus becoming a nationally recognized movement. Since then, many have taken to the streets to protest against the wrongful deaths of more Black lives by police conduct, as well as the lack of accountability held against these officers. Though Black people only make up 13% of the overall US population, they are killed at more than twice the rate than that of a White person. Black people are clearly killed at an inordinate rate.


Since 2015, we've lost over 1,300 African Americans to police shootings. We remember them. We say their names.


Yet these are only a few of many disheartening statistics concerning blatant, anti-Black, and systemic racism. Other examples of systemic racism are reflected in the demographics of US prisons, housing, or should I say gentrification, and most recently the Covid-19 pandemic. The video below goes into greater detail about systemic racism against Black people in the US.


The BLM fight to bring attention to these disparities has incited its expansion. Along with the strong social media presence, Black Lives Matter activists have taken to the streets in massive protests that have taken place worldwide. In the wake of George Floyd’s death, there have been protests in all 50 states in addition to over 70 different countries all over the world (shown below). Black Lives Matter is considered to be the largest movement in US history. This widespread movement has caused many from individuals to billion-dollar corporations to reflect on their actions and how they can make a difference.
